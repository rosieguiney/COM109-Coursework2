    The 4-7-8 breathing technique is a simple relaxation method that helps calm the mind and body. It involves inhaling quietly through the nose for 4 seconds, holding the breath for 7 seconds, and then exhaling slowly through the mouth for 8 seconds. This pattern encourages deeper breathing, reduces stress, and can even help with falling asleep more quickly when practiced regularly.     Box breathing, also known as square breathing, is a technique used to promote focus and relaxation. It consists of inhaling for 4 seconds, holding the breath for 4 seconds, exhaling for 4 seconds, and holding again for 4 seconds before repeating the cycle. This structured rhythm helps regulate breathing, lower anxiety, and improve concentration, making it popular among athletes and professionals in high-pressure situations.
